The origin of coffee dates back to 600 A.D There are several legends about the origin of coffee, one of which states that in Eastern Afica, a shepherd called Kaldi observed that his goats were overactive when they are cherry-red berries from a shrub. He tried to eat some of the berries, and found himself invigorated too. Soon, news spread about the stimulating effects of these berries, and monks started using the berries to stay awake during their long hours of prayers. From Eastern Africa, coffee was transported to Arabia and then to turkey, where they roasted the beans and boiled them and gave rise to a crude from of coffee that we use today. Soon, coffee spread to Europe, America, and other continents. It is one of the most popular beverages enjoyrd by multitudes of people all over the world and is available in different varieties.
